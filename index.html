I'm your Huckleberry.

The initial reaction I get when I tell people I'm going into programming is that of curiosity. I didn't know many developers until I forced my questions and queries on a few new friends over the past year or so. They have been so patient and gracious, helpful and encouraging, but most of all just they get it. They validate my feelings of success and inadequacy, as well as the intense need/want/must of building.

I discovered coding after visiting a friend I hadn't seen in years, who is an amazing full stack developer for a large company. I asked a few random questions enveloped in the same curiosity I now recieve from others, went home and bought Jon Duckett's "HTML and CSS: Design and Build Websites". It was game over for me. I hadn't felt a drive and passion for anything since I began playing sports. I read mutliple books front to back, then began to apply my knowledge. I enjoy looking back at my first site, how sloppy and redundant it was, compared to now when I have a more modular approach. I have a long way to go, endless things to learn, and multiple projects in the works, but I am truly excited for everything to come! Have a look at my GitHub in the navigation to critique my code, I'm always open to suggestions and connections!

Finally and most importantly, whether you're here to scope me out or a friend browsing through, THANK YOU for taking the time to look over my site and projects. If you were more interested in learning about my personality and if I may be a fit for your team, have a look through my photo collage below!
